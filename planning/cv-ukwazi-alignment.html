Gap analysis by theme

T1 Scheduling & sequencing

Gap: No pit pushback, strip/dragline block, or underground ring sequencing; no equipment or geotechnical constraints in mining context. Gym app proves calendar/resource assignment logic only.

Portfolio need: One surface or UG scheduling demo with explicit constraints (fleet, shift, geotech) and KPIs — reframe gym scheduling as supporting analog, not primary evidence (per PR-02 risk note).

T2 DES/ABS simulation

Gap: Published simulations are decision-science tutorials, not truck-shovel vs scraper haulage or contractor coordination. Method is proven; mining domain vocabulary and realistic entities are missing.

Portfolio need: Reframe existing ABS/DES skill toward material-handling or fleet bottleneck scenarios aligned with site services and K-Tec value cases (anchor §5) — domain framing from Wave 1/2 research, not CV alone.

T3 Reconciliation & spatial KPIs

Gap: Baobab work shows ETL → dashboard → accountability reporting for quality audits, not survey-to-plan variance or spatial performance measurement on a mine plan.

Portfolio need: Demo that ties planned vs actual tonnage/grade (or block-level variance) to a reconciliation dashboard; leverage data-pipeline and VBA/dashboard skills with synthetic spatial data (PR-07b).

T4 Techno-economic trade-offs

Gap: No IPCC vs trucking, open-pit-to-underground transition, or feasibility stage-gate models on CV. Decision analysis and OR training are foundational only.

Portfolio need: One trade-off model with two or more method alternatives and explicit NPV/throughput/risk metrics — optional breadth pillar per taxonomy suggested mix (T4 or T6).

T5 Forecasting / MDP / capacity planning

Gap: Honours MDP is healthcare workforce, not mine fleet or crew sizing. Forecasting competency is stated but not tied to a mining case on CV.

Portfolio need: Reframe MDP or forecasting for equipment/crew capacity under uncertainty; strongest transferable proof — lowest lift among “Good fit” themes if mining entities substituted.

T6 Closure & risk-based finance

Gap: Complete absence of closure liability, stochastic provisioning, or valuation examples. Weakest theme on CV (score 1).

Portfolio need: Optional scenario-based closure cost model if breadth desired; otherwise defer to T4 for techno-economic showcase unless PR-19 seeds score highly on differentiation.

Ukwazi role fit (from CV lens)

Anchor §5 positions the Quantitative Planning Analyst role around operational technical services (scheduling, reconciliation, data-driven improvement) plus quantitative feasibility/valuation backbone. CV strengths align best with T2 and T5 delivery mechanics (simulation, optimisation, forecasting) and partial T3 data pipeline skills. T1, T4, and T6 — all high-priority at Ukwazi — require net-new portfolio proof. Generic frontend CV items (ecosystem.Ai, gym app) should support demos, not lead the narrative.
