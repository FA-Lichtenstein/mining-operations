Douglas A. Sloan — Mine Management (2012 reprint)

Task ID: PR-14 · Date: 2026-05-27 · Upstream deps: PR-01 · Source: sources/processed/Douglas A_ Sloan - Mine Management (2012).txt · Themes: T1–T6 · Read depth: deep Ch 1 (planning process, income planning), Ch 6 (MIS, flexible budget, reconciliation variances), Ch 7–9 (labour standards); skim Ch 2–3, 11, 15; selective Ch 10; TOC Ch 4–5, 12–14

Chapman & Hall mine-management text (first published 1983; 2012 Internet Archive reprint used here — content is unchanged). Unlike OR handbooks in Wave 2, Sloan is a native mining management-systems source: four-phase corporate planning (Exhibit 1.1), income planning with mine–mill tonnage reconciliation, and a full management-accounting / MIS stack (custodial vs decision vs management accounting; flexible budgets; method-mix and draw-mix variances; responsibility reporting). Deep read centred on T3 production reconciliation & spatial KPIs and T5 labour-standard / PI control. Five seeds below; top three by composite: A → C → B (all ≥ 3.5). Seed D (3.92) also clears the PR-19 threshold; E (~3.47) is below. Seed A anchors T3 flexible-budget reconciliation; Seed C anchors superintendent-level plan-vs-actual accountability.

1. Scope & audience

  • Text: 15 chapters + index; ~28k sentencized lines. Practical systems for mine managers — planning, organization, MBO, MIS, labour standards, incentive bonus, maintenance. No optimisation theory; complements Hillier (PR-10) and Reid & Sanders (PR-11) with mining-native control vocabulary.
  • Audience: Quantitative Planning Analyst portfolio — citeable reconciliation and variance-decomposition patterns for browser dashboards per PR-04; fills T3 gap left by Hillier (PR-10 has no T3 seeds).
  • Read boundary: Deep Ch 1 (planning process, Eagle Mining case, income planning, ton vs grade); Ch 6 (management accounting, Examples 6.1–6.2, Tables 6.4–6.6, method/draw mix); Ch 7–9 (standard types, establishment methods, PI control). Skim Ch 2 (organization/Urwick), Ch 3 (MBO), Ch 11 (maintenance), Ch 15 (summary). Selective Ch 10 (PI-linked bonus link to Seed E). TOC: Ch 4–5, 12–14.
  • Cross-refs: SA KPI demo site profiles (PR-09); synthetic cost-centre data (PR-07b); Reid SPC reconciliation analogue (PR-11 Seed E); handbook MILP scheduling (PR-13) for T1 contrast; Dunbar value-chain vocabulary (PR-15) for strip ratio, dilution, and cut-off context ahead of MIS reconciliation; SME formula definitions (PR-16) for E=A×U, match factor, and study-stage accuracy bands on fleet/economics seeds.

5. Portfolio seeds

Five seeds tagged T1–T6; chapter citations for each. Rank by composite: A (4.32) → C (4.16) → B (4.08) → D (3.92) → E (3.47). Seeds A–D clear PR-19 threshold (≥ 3.5); E is kept for T5 breadth. A prioritises T3 reconciliation.

Seed A — Flexible budget variance lab T3

Source: Ch 6 Examples 6.1–6.2 (fixed vs flexed cost centre A); Ch 6 § eliminating volume effects; separate breaking/ hauling/ bolting allowances.

Idea: Interactive cost-centre report: toggle fixed vs flexible budget on synthetic tons broken/hauled. Show how volume-favourable mask hides adverse labour, rock-bolt and equipment-downtime variances. Exception-highlight adverse lines; manager “explain variance” notes field — flagship T3 cite for mining MIS culture.

Seed B — Method-mix & draw-mix decomposer T3

Source: Ch 6 Table 6.5–6.6 (jumbo/jackleg/air-trac; CC101 vs CC202 draw); § mining-method and draw-mix variances.

Idea: Two cost-centre toy model: plan assumes jumbo/air-trac mix; user shifts actual methods and draw tonnages. Waterfall chart: total variance → method mix (by centre) → draw mix → net. Distinctive mining reconciliation demo vs generic SPC (PR-11 Seed E).

Seed C — Mine superintendent accountability dashboard T3

Source: Ch 6 Table 6.5 (tons hoisted, grade, pounds, beat costs 110/310/326, method/draw mix lines, fixed centres); Fig 6.3 report hierarchy.

Idea: Single-page monthly MIS: production headline KPIs → beat-level variable efficiency → mix variances → fixed cost centres (maintenance, ventilation). Drill-down to workplace heading report. User role: mine superintendent per PR-09.

Seed D — Mobile equipment availability / utilization tracker T2 T3

Source: Ch 6 Table 6.4 (scooptram pool: 92% avail vs 89% plan; 74% util vs 90%; maintenance vs mining responsibility split).

Idea: Fleet KPI cards: availability (maintenance foreman) vs utilization (mining captain) vs net usage hours. Charge-back at standard $/hour; repair variance by subsystem (engine, transmission, hydraulics). Supports fleet-sizing narrative over rolling quarters.

Seed E — Attainable-standard PI & labour control T5 T3

Source: Ch 7–9 (standard types; PI formula; Figs 9.1–9.2); Ch 10 PI-linked bonus (selective).

Idea: Shift report: standard work completed vs actual time → PI; non-productive delay codes (equipment, materials, coordination). Compare probable (budget) vs attainable (control) standard sliders. Below showcase threshold (~3.47) but pairs with Seed A for T3+T5 pillar story.
