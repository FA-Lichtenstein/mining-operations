Ukwazi — Services Summary

Prepared for portfolio scoping · Source: ukwazi.com (May 2026) · Role target: Quantitative Planning Analyst

Ukwazi (“to know” in Zulu) is a South African-based, Level 2 B-BBEE mining advisory and operations services company, founded in 2004. They position themselves as a multi-disciplinary one-stop shop covering the mining value chain “from mine to market and everything in between.” Clients are major and mid-tier miners across Africa and selected international geographies.

1. Company positioning

  • Core promise: pragmatic, safe, cost-effective, sustainable mining and engineering solutions.
  • Delivery model: integrated project teams built from permanent Superteams in a matrix structure — each Superteam specialises in a narrow band of commodities and mining methods rather than generalist consultants.
  • Geographic focus: sub-Saharan Africa primary; work also in Central/Western Africa, Central Asia, Europe, and the Americas depending on method and commodity.
  • Scale: one of the largest specialist mining service providers in Africa; projects range from small ADT operations to pits moving ~1 Mt/day of rock.

2. Six advisory service pillars

The website organises advisory work into six main areas. Below is what each actually covers.

2.1 Mining

End-to-end mining engineering across surface and underground methods, organised by Superteam specialisation.

Surface mining
Open-pit and related surface methods from large polymetallic pits (up to ~900 m depth, irregular geometry, high selectivity) down to medium/small ADT-based operations. Work includes pit/pushback design, waste dump and haul road layout, equipment selection, blasting and grade control, IPCC and trolley-assist trade-offs, and energy-cost-sensitive haulage optimisation.
Agricultural minerals & coal
Design, scheduling, and operational support for potash, phosphates, coal (thermal and metallurgical), anthracite, salt, and similar bulk minerals. Sub-areas:
  • Strip mining — horizontal progression, waste rollover/backfill, techno-economic pit limits (proprietary tools + Whittle).
  • Draglines — bench/pass configuration, block-by-block life-of-mine performance modelling (proprietary software), short/medium/long-term budget alignment.
  • Soft-rock underground — continuous miner bord-and-pillar, pillar extraction/stooping, longwall/shortwall where geotechnically suitable.
Underground mining (hard rock)
Method selection and design across five Superteam niches; notable specialty in open-pit to underground transition:
  • Massive mining — open stoping variants, AVOCA, cut-and-fill, room-and-pillar; steep and flat dipping bodies.
  • Caving — block caving and sublevel caving for high-production underground operations (kimberlite, massive hard-rock bodies).
  • Mechanised room & pillar — flat tabular bodies > ~1.6 m (BIC, Great Dyke, Northern Cape manganese, Copperbelt, West African gold/manganese).
  • Conventional narrow tabular — labour-intensive 1.0–1.8 m cuts (PGMs, chrome, gold/uranium reefs).
  • Hybrid narrow tabular — partial mechanisation of material handling with layout/ventilation trade-offs.
Site services (operational)
On-mine teams that help clients operate what Ukwazi designs. This is the closest published match to a “quantitative planning analyst” remit:
  • Short-term mine planning, scheduling, and sequencing.
  • Production process optimisation and real-time operational adjustments.
  • Surveying and reconciliation.
  • Contractor/owner alignment, spatial performance measurement, data-driven improvement.

2.2 Bankable feasibility

Project development from opportunity identification through constructable, fundable studies.

  • Feasibility studies: concept → prefeasibility → definitive/bankable feasibility; stage-gate guidance aligned with AACE cost classes and SAMREC/SAMVAL/SAMESG (also JORC, NI 43-101).
  • Integrated project management & controls: scope/quality/cost/schedule integration; managing internal and external specialist teams.
  • Multi-disciplinary studies: orchestrating geology, mining, geotechnical, infrastructure, processing, tailings/waste, ESG, and water disciplines.
  • Techno-economic trade-offs: mine-to-market configuration, accelerated vs. full stage-gate development paths, lender/investor support.

2.3 Engineering infrastructure

  • Surface water management & design — clean/dirty water separation, pollution control dams, piping, recycling, regulatory compliance.
  • Underground infrastructure design — shafts/access trade-offs, hoisting, conveyors/chutes, backfill plants, ventilation, services (water, compressed air, power, comms), personnel transport, refuge chambers, workshops.
  • Mining infrastructure implementation — haul roads, bridges, dams, pumps; 3D/BIM coordination; commissioning and close-out.
  • HME studies — fleet selection, maintenance system design, strategy optimisation, monitoring, mentoring.

2.4 Sustainable mining

  • ESG permitting & authorisation — ESG strategy, risk assessment, IFC/Equator/GRI-aligned reporting, audits, permit acquisition, EIAs, social licence, post-approval compliance.
  • Water management — lifecycle water balance, treatment, tailings-related water control, monitoring and reporting.
  • Mine closure — closure design for post-mining land use, risk-based closure cost estimates with financial modelling, strategies to limit closure liabilities, execution and community engagement.

2.5 Transaction advisory & corporate services

Expanded via acquisition of Sidus Consulting (2022) into the Ukwazi Transaction Advisory (UTA) division.

  • Due diligence — four-level matrix of review depth across geology, mining, plant, infrastructure, ESG, permitting, and financial performance; handles distressed/early-stage data gaps.
  • Independent technical reports — CPRs, MERs, PEAs, ITRs; SAMREC/JORC/NI 43-101 compliant public reporting.
  • Mine valuation reports — acquisitions/disposals, finance raising, corporate reporting; competent/independent valuator work under international guidelines.
  • Financial modelling (UTA/Sidus capability, referenced in industry press) — capital budgeting, closure liabilities, IPO support, techno-economic assessments, production and cost forecasting.

2.6 Operations & equipment (beyond pure advisory)

TseboKgadi (contract mining & civil works)
Black female-led operations arm (Mpumalanga & Northern Cape): rehabilitation, small-to-medium surface mining (up to ~300 000 BCM/month), haul road maintenance, stockpile/materials handling. Emphasis on operational excellence, H&S, sustainability, and data-driven approaches.
K-Tec equipment (exclusive sub-Saharan distributor)
ADT pull scrapers for dry mining and tailings/residue rehandling. Value proposition: ~50% less horsepower and crew vs. truck-and-shovel for suitable material, lower fuel/emissions, reduced water vs. hydrological mining, continuous load-haul-dump operation. Product lines include direct-mount scrapers, train configurations, ejector bodies, land levelers.

4. Industry trends Ukwazi highlights

  • Pressure to reduce water, diesel, and emissions (driving dry mining methods, K-Tec adoption, ESG integration).
  • Reprocessing of tailings and residue deposits.
  • Open-pit-to-underground transitions as pits deepen.
  • Clients sometimes compress feasibility stages — Ukwazi advises on risk when skipping stage gates.
  • Increased sophistication required for closure planning and financial provisioning.
  • Post-pandemic capital allocation and M&A activity requiring rigorous financial modelling and due diligence.

5. Portfolio alignment guide

For a Quantitative Planning Analyst application, the strongest published fit is operational technical services (scheduling, reconciliation, data-driven improvement) plus the quantitative backbone of feasibility, valuation, and proprietary mine-planning software work elsewhere in the firm.

High-priority portfolio themes

  • Strong fit Short- to medium-term mine scheduling & sequencing — pit pushbacks, strip/dragline blocks, underground production rings; demonstrate optimisation under equipment and geotechnical constraints.
  • Strong fit Discrete-event / agent-based simulation — truck-shovel vs. scraper systems, haulage bottlenecks, contractor coordination, material handling (aligns with site services and K-Tec value cases).
  • Strong fit Production reconciliation & performance measurement — spatial KPIs, survey-to-plan variance, dashboards; mirrors their emphasis on transparency and accountability on site.
  • Strong fit Techno-economic trade-off models — method selection (e.g. IPCC vs. trucking, open pit vs. underground transition timing), aligned with feasibility and dragline/strip optimisation narratives.
  • Good fit Forecasting & MDP/optimisation under capacity constraints — workforce/equipment planning analogues to mine fleet and crew sizing; connects to honours MDP work if reframed for mining context.
  • Good fit Closure cost / risk-based financial modelling — stochastic or scenario-based closure liability estimates; ties to sustainable mining and UTA valuation work.

Secondary themes (differentiating but not core to the role)

  • ESG/permitting workflows — better as supporting context than primary OR showcase.
  • Detailed geotechnical or ventilation engineering — outside typical OR scope unless coupled with optimisation.
  • Pure infrastructure BIM/3D — engineering-heavy; mention integration skill, don’t lead with it.

Suggested portfolio framing

Lead with operational planning and quantitative decision support, not generic software CV items:

  1. One scheduling/optimisation case (surface or underground, with clear constraints and KPIs).
  2. One simulation case comparing alternatives (equipment, method, or contractor strategy).
  3. One reconciliation or forecasting case showing data pipeline → insight → decision.
  4. Optional: a feasibility-style trade-off or closure/valuation scenario model showing stage-gate thinking.

Use commodity contexts Ukwazi actually serves: coal/phosphate strip mines, polymetallic open pits, PGM/narrow tabular underground, or tailings/residue rehandling — rather than abstract manufacturing examples.
