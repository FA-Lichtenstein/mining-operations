3. Value-chain narrative

End-to-end metal supply as Dunbar frames it — the story arc for demo onboarding and hiring conversations.

  1. Discovery & definition (Ch 1): Low crustal concentrations → exploration toolkit → drill core → 3-D ore-body and block models. Planning inputs: grade, tonnage, geometry, hardness proxies.
  2. Extraction (Ch 2): Open pit (ore + waste + tailings facility + plant) or underground (shaft/decline, stopes, minimal waste rock). Material flows governed by strip ratio, mining method (Table 2.4 cost/recovery/flexibility), and the cyclic bottleneck at drill + load-haul-dump. Grade control limits dilution (internal vs external).
  3. Beneficiation (Ch 3 skim): Comminution → separation → concentrate or doré. Recovery and concentration factor feed economic cut-off calculations.
  4. Waste & water (Ch 5): Tailings (often largest long-term liability), waste rock (ARD risk), wastewater, acid drainage. Management plan required pre-permit; Equity Silver shows post-closure treatment ~US$2.065M/yr (2009–2013 avg) decades after mining.
  5. Social licence (Ch 6): Safety culture, CSR continuum, IMAs/IBAs, physical + biodiversity + climate footprint. Scope 1–3 emissions along the value chain; mining ~4–10% of global GHG (Scope 1+2) but Scope 3 dominates for steel/clean-energy metals.
  6. Economics & reporting (Ch 7): MineCo → RefineCo → exchange/traders; resources (potential) vs reserves (extractable today); JORC/CRIRSCO/NI 43-101; cut-off grade ties block model to mine life and capacity sizing.
  7. Future paradigm (Ch 8): Lower discovery rates, NZE metal demand, automation/sorting/small fleets, biotech and digital twins — or a virtual, modular metal supply network (Fig 8.9).

5. Hiring-manager & demo language

Phrases grounded in Dunbar that signal mining fluency in interviews and portfolio README copy.

  • “I frame short-term plans in the mining cycle — drill and load-haul-dump are the high-cost phases that constrain shift throughput.” (Ch 2 Fig 2.18)
  • “Material movement is driven by strip ratio and pushback sequencing — e.g. Bagdad at 1.4, Cortez dewatering at 100,000 L/min.” (Ch 2 Table 2.1; Fig 2.30)
  • “Plan vs actual reconciliation needs dilution control language — internal vs external dilution and effective grade g×(1−d).” (Ch 2)
  • “Cut-off grade links the block model to mine life: too low overstates reserves and oversizes plant; too high strands economic ore.” (Ch 7; Fig 7.11)
  • “Closure isn’t optional — ARD treatment at Equity Silver still runs ~US$2.065M/yr (2009–2013 avg) with 4,000 t lime annually, decades after stop.” (Ch 5)
  • “I distinguish resources (potential) from reserves (extractable under NI 43-101/JORC) when discussing mine-life scenarios.” (Ch 7)
  • “Energy-transition planning should reference Scope 1–3 along the metal value chain, not pit diesel alone.” (Ch 6)
  • “Autonomous haulage and ore sorting are capital-productivity levers — 20–50% waste rejection before the mill.” (Ch 8 Paths 1–2)
