Hillier & Lieberman — Introduction to Operations Research (2024)

Task ID: PR-10 · Date: 2026-05-27 · Upstream deps: PR-01 · Source: sources/processed/Frederick Hillier, Gerald Lieberman - Introduction to Operations Research-McGraw Hill (2024).txt · Themes: T1–T6 · Read depth: deep Ch 3–4, 7, 10–12, 14, 17, 19–20, 22; TOC skim elsewhere

The 2024 release of Hillier & Lieberman remains the canonical graduate OR textbook: LP/simplex (Ch 3–4), uncertainty and recourse (Ch 7), network and integer models (Ch 10–12), metaheuristics (Ch 14), queueing and simulation (Ch 17, 20), MDPs (Ch 19), and PERT/CPM (Ch 22). Duality and alternative LP algorithms (Ch 5–6, 8–9) were TOC-skimmed only. The text contains no mining case studies; portfolio value comes from method transfer — especially queueing + DES for haulage (Ch 17, 20), network flow for material routing (Ch 10), equipment-replacement MDPs (Ch 19), two-stage stochastic LP (Ch 7.6), and fixed-charge method selection (Ch 12.3). Five seeds below map these chapters to Ukwazi themes; top three score composite ≥ 3.5 on the PR-19 formula.

1. Scope & audience

  • Text: 11th edition, 2024 release (McGraw Hill). ~118k sentencized lines; full LP/network/simulation/MDP coverage plus website supplements (Ch 18 inventory, Ch 21–28).

  • Cross-refs: External mining OR cases in PR-05; SA KPI context in PR-09; synthetic haulage data in PR-07b.

5. Portfolio seeds

Five seeds tagged T1–T6; chapter/section cited for each. Gorai in-book cases remain reserved for PR-17. Seeds D and E score below the PR-19 showcase threshold (composite ~3.16 and ~3.77); retained for T4/T1 breadth.

Seed A — Shovel–truck queue DES + fleet sizing T2 T5

Source: Ch 17.1–17.3 (queue structure), 17.6 (M/M/s), 17.10 (minimise E(TC) = Css + CwL); Ch 20.1–20.2 (DES when queue formulas insufficient).

Idea: Model a loading point as M/M/s (s = shovels or berths), then wrap in discrete-event simulation for non-exponential load times, breakdowns, and blended fleets. Compare deterministic queue formulas vs simulated cycle times — mirrors PR-05 Peru fleet DES (#01).

Seed B — Haul-network min-cost / max-flow dispatcher T1 T2

Source: Ch 10.2–10.6 (network terminology, shortest path, max flow, min cost flow); Ch 9.1 (transportation as LP special case).

Idea: Pit → stockpile → plant as min-cost flow; haul-road segments as capacitated arcs (max-flow subproblem for shift tonnage target). CSX rail vignette (Ch 10.6) validates network OR at industrial scale.

Seed C — Haul-truck replacement MDP T5

Source: Ch 19.1 (four-state machine deterioration, replace at state 3), 19.2–19.3 (MDP formulation, LP for optimal policy), 19.4 (practice).

Idea: Weekly inspection states for CAT 793-class units; actions = operate, repair, replace; transition matrix from synthetic PM history. Policy heatmap and expected cost vs age — complements PR-05 equipment SLP (#09) with MDP framing.

Seed D — Fixed-charge IPCC vs trucking selector T4

Source: Ch 12.3 (fixed-charge yj binary), Ch 3.3–3.4 (LP assumptions, pollution abatement mix); Ch 7.1 (sensitivity on chosen mix).

Idea: Binary activate IPCC corridor (setup capex + fixed OPEX) vs variable trucking cost per tonne-km. Small BIP solvable in browser; sensitivity on fuel/energy price — aligns PR-05 IPCC DES (#02) with explicit investment gate.

Seed E — Pushback PERT with simulated deadline risk T1

Source: Ch 22.1–22.5 (Reliable Construction CPM/PERT, time-cost crash); Ch 10.8 (network time-cost); Ch 20.2 (simulate project deadline probability vs PERT approximations).

Idea: Network of pushback activities (drill, blast, load, rehandle) with three-point durations; critical path + optional crash; Monte Carlo completion before LOM milestone — SA context from PR-09 strip-ratio KPIs.
