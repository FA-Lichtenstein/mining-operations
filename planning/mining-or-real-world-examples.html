Mining OR Real-World Examples

Task ID: PR-05 · Date: 2026-05-27 · Upstream deps: PR-01, PR-02, PR-03, PR-04 · Themes: T1–T6

Twenty external mining operations-research cases from peer-reviewed literature and industry practice, each tagged to portfolio themes T1–T6 and rated for cloneability on an Angular+Firebase demo stack (1–5). Gorai & Chatterjee (2022) in-book case studies are intentionally excluded — those are reserved for PR-17. Cloneability reflects browser-feasibility with synthetic data per PR-04: DES/MDP dashboards score highest; large MIP pit optimisers score lowest.

Scope and method

  • Boundary: External academic/industry sources only; no duplication of Gorai textbook cases (Indonesian coal LPP, Indian iron-ore MILP, copper-cobalt DP, conveyor reliability, shovel-truck queuing, African non-linear scheduling).
  • Cloneability (1–5): How readily the case becomes a polished Angular+Firebase portfolio module using synthetic CSV/GeoJSON and Web Workers — not whether the original authors used that stack.
  • Theme tags: Primary theme first; secondary where the case spans domains (see theme-taxonomy.md).
  • Research date: May 2026 web/literature search; URLs verified at authoring time. Case #05 is industry practice (not peer-reviewed); labelled accordingly in detail.

Case studies (detail)

01 — Peru open-pit copper fleet DES T2 T5 · Clone 5

Source: Huayanca, Bujaico & Delgado, Appl. Sci. 2023 — MDPI. Stochastic DES (Arena) calibrates a 1-day operation then scales to annual plan for a 4500 m Peru copper mine expanding from 100 kt/d to 140 kt/d; DES required more trucks and longer queues vs deterministic planning.

Portfolio hook: Flagship T2 Worker DES — queue charts, fleet count slider, deterministic vs stochastic toggle.

02 — Iron-ore IPCC vs truck-shovel DES T2 T4 · Clone 5

Source: Zamani et al., APCOM 2024 — U Alberta MOL. SimPy DES of truck-shovel, IPCC, and OPCC scenarios on an active iron-ore pit; validated against operational data; scenario panel compares OPEX, throughput, and timeline.

Portfolio hook: Combined T2+T4 module — scenario switcher + cost waterfall chart (PR-04 WSM fork for method selection).

03 — Haulage cycle KPI alert + DES T2 T3 · Clone 5

Source: Coronado & Tenorio (2015), SME/APCOM; extended in Fahl (2023) — MOL paper; Arizona mine thesis — UA repository. Context-based alert system flags sub-standard load-haul-dump cycles; Arena DES compares As-Is vs To-Be routes.

Portfolio hook: Bridges T2 simulation with T3 KPI dashboard — cycle-time heatmap + alert thresholds.

04 — Proactive mine-to-mill reconciliation (Brazil) T3 · Clone 5

Source: Campos et al., J. Rock Mech. Geotech. Eng. 2017 — ScienceDirect. Copper/gold operation monitors reconciliation indicators (PR, MR, etc.) as KPIs; quarterly matrices feed LOM forecasts.

Portfolio hook: Chart.js reconciliation matrix + stage-to-stage variance table; synthetic tonnage/grade CSV.

05 — Mine-to-mill control charts & TARPs T3 · Clone 5

Source: Appian Capital Advisory industry note — Appian insights. Three-month weighted rolling averages and control charts distinguish noise from actionable variance; trigger action response plans (TARPs).

Portfolio hook: Lightweight SPC dashboard — no pit optimiser required; strong Ukwazi site-services narrative.

06 — MIP truck dispatch validated by pit DES T1 T2 · Clone 4

Source: Smith, Linderoth & Luedtke (2020) — Optimization Online. Nonlinear flow-rate policy vs rolling-horizon MIP dispatch; full DES output analysis shows MIP policy consistently beats average-flow heuristics.

Portfolio hook: Toy dispatch grid + Worker DES replay; pre-compute MIP results for large instances (PR-04 anti-pattern guard).

07 — Sungun copper real-time multi-objective dispatch T1 T2 · Clone 4

Source: Mohtasham et al., Optim. Eng. 2022 — Springer. Two-stage allocation planning + dynamic truck assignment; DES of Iranian copper mine benchmarks heuristics vs multi-objective model.

Portfolio hook: Dispatch rule comparator UI; map shovel/dump nodes on simplified pit graph.

08 — Bauxite semi-mobile IPCC vs truck haulage T4 · Clone 4

Source: Ben-Awuah & Hosseini, APCOM 2017 (MOL) — U Alberta MOL. 47-year mine life; IPCC cuts life-of-mine haulage cost ~60%, ~$1.33/t saving vs truck-shovel despite higher CAPEX.

Portfolio hook: NPV/LCC calculator with CAPEX/OPEX sliders — ideal WSM-style T4 demo per PR-04.

09 — Loader/truck selection + replacement SLP T5 · Clone 4

Source: Albor & Fioroni, Int. J. Mining Sci. Technol. 2014 — Tecnalia repository. Stochastic LP integrates fleet selection and replacement under stochastic production-rate constraint; robust vs deterministic solutions.

Portfolio hook: Fleet mix table + scenario tree for availability; aligns with CV MDP/T5 proof (PR-03).

10 — Probabilistic closure liability framework T6 · Clone 4

Source: Botes, ACG Mine Closure 2024 — ACG papers. Monte Carlo over closure actions/components with probability-weighted options; outputs cost distribution not single-point estimate.

Portfolio hook: Scenario histogram + exceedance curve; component toggle panel — no spatial pit model needed.

11 — Closure decision tree + Monte Carlo exceedance T6 · Clone 4

Source: Errington & Scott, CIM/MEND closure costing — UBC library. Decision tree over closure element combinations; Monte Carlo on individual scenarios; 90% confidence cost vs most-likely case illustrated for high/low risk sites.

Portfolio hook: Tree visualisation + cost-probability chart; pairs with T6 gap noted in PR-03.

12 — CADSIM dragline strip-mine planning T1 T2 · Clone 4

Source: CADSIM dragline simulation study, Australian coal strip mining — Academia. CADSIM integrates geology into dragline method/layout/pit geometry selection; case studies show strip-ratio sensitivity to haul-road width and bench design.

Portfolio hook: Strip block schedule + dragline cycle DES; aligns with Ukwazi coal/phosphate strip context (anchor §5).

13 — Three-stage truck dispatch (network LP + DP) T1 · Clone 3

Source: Yang et al., J. Applied Mathematics 2015 (extends White & Olson) — Wiley. Shortest paths → material-flow LP → truck assignment DP; reported 10–20% productivity gains in literature benchmarks.

Portfolio hook: Small pit network graph; stage outputs as educational pipeline — MIP size limits clone score.

14 — Holingol autonomous coal truck MILP T1 · Clone 3

Source: Li et al., Front. Eng. Manag. 2025 — HEP journal. Branch-and-price MILP for autonomous haulage with coal blending/calorific balance; real Inner Mongolia mine data.

Portfolio hook: Blending constraint story strong for interviews; exact solver needs Cloud Function or pre-baked solutions.

15 — OP–UG transition depth (integer programming) T4 · Clone 3

Source: Ataee-Pour et al., J. S. Afr. Inst. Min. Metall. 2016 — SCIELO SA. IP on 3D gold deposit selects transition level (e.g. level 7, 140 m) beating traditional stripping-ratio rule; crown-pillar levels enforced.

Portfolio hook: 2D level cross-section + NPV by transition depth chart; relevant SA mining literature (feeds PR-09).

16 — Stochastic OP–UG transition scheduling T4 T5 · Clone 2

Source: De La Vergne, Optim. Eng. 2017 — Springer. Two-stage SIP generates OP and UG schedules per candidate transition depth under geological uncertainty; picks depth maximising combined NPV.

Portfolio hook: Conceptual scenario explorer only unless PR-07b synthetic block models supplied.

17 — Joint stochastic short-term schedule + fleet management T1 T5 · Clone 2

Source: Goodfellow & Dimitrakopoulos, Optim. Eng. 2020 — Springer. Simultaneous shovel relocation, heterogeneous truck assignment, and multi-destination material routing for a gold mining complex under equipment uncertainty.

Portfolio hook: Method reference for PR-17/Gorai contrast; full model too large for browser demo.

18 — Kiruna iron-ore underground MILP aggregation T1 · Clone 2

Source: Newman et al., EJOR 2007 — ScienceDirect. Machine-placement start-time MILP with time-period aggregation heuristic; Swedish Kiruna mine instances.

Portfolio hook: UG ring/place timeline UI possible; solver backend required for credibility.

19 — US gold mine stochastic RCPSP T1 T5 · Clone 2

Source: Nesbitt et al., EJOR 2021 — author PDF. Stochastic IP on ~15.7k UG activities (1.8 Mt/a, 370 koz Au); duration/value uncertainty; tactical vs deterministic RCPSP gap.

Portfolio hook: Small RCPSP toy in Worker feasible; full case needs cloud MIP — partial clone only.

20 — Potash block selection & sequencing MILP T1 · Clone 3

Source: Schmidt et al., Math. Prog. Comp. 2021 — Springer. Quality-target deviation minimisation for flat-bedded UG potash; MILP + constructive heuristic (+92.5% improvement on hard instances).

Portfolio hook: Quality-band scheduling grid — medium-size MILP; synthetic block model from PR-07b could help.
