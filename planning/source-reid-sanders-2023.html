Reid & Sanders — Operations Management: An Integrated Approach (2023)

Task ID: PR-11 · Date: 2026-05-27 · Upstream deps: PR-01 · Source: sources/processed/R. Dan Reid, Nada R. Sanders - Operations Management_ An Integrated Approach (2023, Wiley).txt · Themes: T1–T6 · Read depth: deep Ch 5–6, 8, 12–13, 15; TOC skim Ch 1–4, 7, 9–11, 14, 16 and supplements

The eighth edition (Wiley, 2023) is an undergraduate–graduate OM survey: strategy and SCM (Ch 2–4), quality and SPC (Ch 5–6), lean (Ch 7), then the planning stack — forecasting (Ch 8), capacity (Ch 9), inventory (Ch 12), sales and operations planning / aggregate planning (Ch 13), MRP/ERP (Ch 14), and scheduling with OPT/TOC (Ch 15). The text has no mining case studies; portfolio value is operational planning vocabulary for browser demos — especially demand forecasting with accuracy control (Ch 8), S&OP hierarchy and level/chase/hybrid aggregate plans (Ch 13), shop/bottleneck scheduling (Ch 15), and SPC as a reconciliation analogue for plan-vs-actual KPIs (Ch 6). Five seeds map these chapters to Ukwazi themes; top three composites are ≥ 3.5 on the PR-19 formula.

5. Portfolio seeds

Five seeds tagged T1–T6; chapter citations for each. Seeds D and E score below the PR-19 showcase threshold (composite ~3.54 and ~3.47); retained for T5/T3 breadth.

Seed A — ROM tonnage forecast lab (MAPE + tracking signal) T5

Source: Ch 8.1–8.4 (principles, MA, exponential & trend-adjusted smoothing, seasonality); Ch 8.7 (MAD, MSE, tracking signal ±4 rule).

Idea: Upload synthetic monthly ROM tonnes (PR-07b); compare naive, moving average, and exponential smoothing; plot actual vs forecast and compute MAD/MSE/tracking signal to trigger model review — mirrors monthly mine-plan forecast governance.

Seed B — S&OP aggregate plan simulator (level vs chase) T1 T5

Source: Ch 13.1 (S&OP, Fig 13.1 hierarchy); Ch 13.2 (demand/capacity options); Ch 13.3 (level, chase, hybrid strategies).

Idea: Six- to twelve-period demand series for pit tonnes; user toggles level plan (inventory/backorder swing) vs chase (overtime/temp labour); chart workforce, stockpile, and cost proxies — G’s Fresh/UPS vignettes as narrative anchors.

Seed C — Bottleneck shift scheduler (SPT + TOC) T1

Source: Ch 15.3–15.6 (Gantt, priority rules, flow time/tardiness); Ch 15.7–15.8 (OPT principles, Table 15.6).

Idea: Small job-shop: shovel maintenance tasks or truck service jobs at one constraint work center; compare SPT vs due-date rules on mean flow time and tardiness; highlight bottleneck utilization — complements Hillier queue DES (PR-10 Seed A) with deterministic sequencing.

Seed D — Pit spares ABC–EOQ dashboard T5

Source: Ch 12.4 (ABC analysis); Ch 12.5 (EOQ assumptions, Q formula, total cost curve).

Idea: Synthetic spares usage table; Pareto ABC chart and EOQ slider for top A-items (tyres, liners). Lower differentiation than Seeds A–C; useful CV breadth for inventory literacy.

Seed E — Grade/recovery SPC control chart T3

Source: Ch 6.2–6.4 (common vs assignable causes); Ch 6.5–6.6 (x̄-R chart logic); Ch 6.8 (process capability).

Idea: Stream of daily mill recovery or truck cycle-time samples vs plan target; x̄ chart with control limits, flag assignable causes when points exceed limits — operational analogue to production reconciliation dashboards (survey vs plan variance).
