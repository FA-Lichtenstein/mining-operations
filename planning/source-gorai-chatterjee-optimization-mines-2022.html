CRC Press textbook (~54k sentencized lines) that is the portfolio’s highest-priority mining-method source alongside the Natural Resources Handbook (PR-13): every major OR technique is taught with in-book mine case studies (Indonesia coal LP, Indian iron-ore transport, southeastern India open-pit MIP schedule, Central Africa copper UPL by DP, Odisha shovel-truck queuing). Five seeds below; top three by composite: A (3.94) → B (3.93) → C (3.55) (all ≥ 3.5). Seed A anchors T1 with $898M NPV MIP case; Seed B anchors T2 with field-data queuing.

1. Scope & audience

  • Text: Gorai & Chatterjee, CRC/Taylor & Francis 2022 (1st ed. 2023 imprint). Eleven chapters from mine systems engineering through nonlinear scheduling; appendices for ZIMPL/CPLEX (Ch 5), Matlab UPL (Ch 6), reliability/inventory tooling.
  • Audience: Quantitative Planning Analyst portfolio — step-by-step formulations, published numeric results, and solver workflows (ZIMPL, CPLEX academic, Excel Solver, Matlab) for browser demos per PR-04; complements Hillier method theory (PR-10) and handbook deployed MILP narratives (PR-13).
  • Read boundary: Deep Ch 3.6 (coal LP); Ch 4.3–4.5 (transport + Indian iron-ore network); Ch 5.3–5.4 (Gomory, B&B, MIP schedule); Ch 6.4–6.6 (2-D UPL, stope DP, copper case); Ch 7.3–7.4 (CPM/PERT, crashing); Ch 10.6–10.8 (M/M/s, machine-repair, shovel-truck). Skim Ch 1–2 (systems, probability); Ch 8.11 (reliability case); Ch 9 (inventory); Ch 11.4 (nonlinear open-pit schedule with stockpile).
  • PR-05 boundary: In-book cases only — Indonesian coal LP (Ch 3.6), Indian iron-ore transport (Ch 4.5), SE India open-pit MIP (Ch 5.4), Central Africa Cu-Co DP UPL (Ch 6.6), Odisha shovel-truck queuing (Ch 10.8), Africa MIQP schedule (Ch 11.4 skim), Ch 8.11 reliability skim. Not duplicated in PR-05 external catalog.
  • Cross-refs: Synthetic block models (PR-07b); SA/India personas (PR-09); external haulage DES (PR-05); Dunbar process context (PR-15).

Seed A — Open-pit MIP production schedule (India iron ore) T1

Source: Ch 5.4 (MIP max NPV Eq. 5.13; reserve Eq. 5.14; slope Eq. 5.15; soft mining/processing/metal penalties); Ch 5 App. (ZIMPL+CPLEX); cites Chatterjee & Dimitrakopoulos (2020) on infeasible pure-IP schedules.

Seed B — Shovel-truck queuing fleet sizing (Odisha) T2

Source: Ch 10.8 ((M/M/1):FCFS/K/K machine-repair model); Ch 10.6.4 formulas; Table 10.5 field inter-arrival/service sample; Figs 10.13–10.18.

Idea: Replay Table 10.5 data in a Worker: sweep trucks N=1…22, plot ηs, Ct, Qn. Highlight optimum at 5 trucks (ηs=100%, Ct≈US$1.32/t vs 22-truck fleet Ct≈US$4.86/t). Pairs Hillier queueing theory (PR-10) with mining-native field data — flagship T2 cite.

Seed C — Dynamic programming ultimate pit (Central Africa copper) T1 T4

Source: Ch 6.4 (2-D UPL recursion); Ch 6.6 (section-wise DP, stitch caveat); App. 6 Matlab; Table 6.3 economics (45° slope, US$1.20/t mining, 98% recovery).

Idea: 2-D block grid section viewer: run DP column stages in TypeScript; show pit envelope vs naive greedy shell. Narrate 3-D stitch limitation and contrast Lerchs–Grossmann / handbook max-closure (PR-13 Ch 29.3). Anchor: 9.1 Mt @ 2.03% Cu, SR 2.22.

Seed D — Multi-echelon iron-ore transport network T2 T4

Source: Ch 4.5 (nine faces, dual crushers, truck/rail stockpiles, six steel plants); Table 4.43 distances; Excel Solver optimum US$596,650/day.

Idea: Interactive flow diagram: adjust face production and route flows; solve min-cost transport LP in Worker. SA/India logistics persona (PR-09). Below PR-19 threshold but strong regional differentiation.

Seed E — Coal seam allocation LP (Indonesia) T4

Source: Ch 3.6 (12 seams × 3 power plants; quality constraints TM/ash/TS/CV; Tables 3.14–3.17); ZIMPL+CPLEX profit US$435,000/day.

Idea: Small LP demo: sliders for seam quality and plant caps; dual-price panel on sulfur constraint. Fast Stack fit (Effort S) — good teaching module, lower composite.
