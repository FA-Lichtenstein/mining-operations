SA Mining Context

Task ID: PR-09 · Date: 2026-05-27 · Upstream deps: PR-01, PR-02 · Themes: T1–T6 · Related: PR-07 (data sources), Ukwazi anchor · Out of scope: CV ↔ Ukwazi matrix (PR-03)

South Africa remains a globally significant minerals producer — dominant in PGMs and ferroalloy feedstocks, still material in gold and coal — governed by the DMPR (successor to DMRE) under the MPRDA, with public resource and reserve reporting under SAMREC and monetary valuation under SAMVAL. Portfolio demos should anchor on this commodity mix, regulatory vocabulary, and the operational KPIs that Ukwazi-style planning and reconciliation work actually influences: plan attainment, strip ratio, fleet utilisation, mine-call factors, and cost per tonne — not generic OR toy problems divorced from Bushveld UG, Northern Cape bulk, or Mpumalanga coal context.

Scope and method

  • Audience: Portfolio builders targeting Ukwazi / SA mining employers; interview and demo narrative layer.
  • Boundary: Commodities, sector structure, regulatory acronyms (SAMREC/SAMVAL/DMPR), and KPI vocabulary. CV skill mapping is PR-03; open-data URLs and ETL patterns are PR-07; synthetic seeds are PR-07b.
  • Research date: 2026-05-27. Production/sales figures cite Stats SA mining production index weights and Minerals Council / DMPR 2024–2025 publications unless noted.
  • Geography: National SA context with provincial anchors where they explain demo commodity choice (Limpopo/NW PGMs, Northern Cape iron/manganese, Mpumalanga coal, Gauteng/Free State gold legacy).

Sector snapshot

Mining contributed roughly 5.8% of nominal GDP (~R439 bn) in 2025 (Minerals Council Facts & Figures; DMPR portal often rounds to ~6%). Direct employment is on the order of 469 000–477 000 depending on source and annualisation (Minerals Council vs DMPR portal, 2025). More than 1 700 operating mines extract 50+ commodity types. The sector is export-heavy: PGMs, gold, iron ore, and coal historically dominate sales and employment; chrome and manganese add bulk-ferroalloy leverage tied to Chinese steel demand.

2025 production narrative (Stats SA / Minerals Council): headline mining production grew only 0.1% y/y — a slowdown from 0.6% in 2024. Bulk and “other metallic” minerals gained (iron ore +3.0%, manganese +5.0%, chromium +3.9%) while traditional anchors softened (PGMs −4.1 to −4.4%, gold −1.7 to −1.9%, coal −0.2 to −0.7%). Higher PGM and gold prices still lifted mineral sales (gold sales +29.7% to ~R185 bn; PGMs +19.5% to ~R206.7 bn in rand terms), decoupling revenue from volume — a useful demo talking point for T3 reconciliation and T4 trade-off stories.

Provincial anchors (demo setting)

  • Limpopo / North West / Gauteng fringe: Bushveld Igneous Complex — PGM, chrome, vanadium, nickel; Merensky and UG2 reefs; concentrator pipelines.
  • Northern Cape: Iron ore (Sishen/Kolomela), manganese (Hotazel), zinc-lead (Gamsberg); open-pit, rail logistics.
  • Mpumalanga: Coal (Witbank/Highveld coalfields); dragline and truck-shovel open cast.
  • Free State / Gauteng: Declining but high-cost deep gold; legacy tailings and closure (T6).
  • KwaZulu-Natal: Anthracite, heavy mineral sands; smaller but diverse.

Regulatory and institutional context

DMRE → DMPR split

The former Department of Mineral Resources and Energy (DMRE) was split after the May 2024 elections. Proclamation No. 179 of 2024 (Gazette 51140, 27 Aug 2024) assigned mining, minerals, and upstream petroleum to the new Department of Mineral and Petroleum Resources (DMPR); electricity and energy moved to a separate department. Legacy dmre.gov.za now redirects to dmpr.gov.za. Energy portfolio items (e.g. fuel price announcements) still appear on DMPR media channels — do not conflate with mine planning regulation in demos.

DMPR
Primary regulator: licensing, mine health and safety coordination, production statistics, transformation charter oversight. Mandate rooted in Constitution s24 and the Mineral and Petroleum Resources Development Act (MPRDA), 2002.
MPRDA
Governs prospecting/mining rights, social and labour plans (SLPs), environmental authorisations interface, and ministerial cadastre. No public bulk rights API — see PR-07 (SAMRAD legacy, Project Indwe phased rollout).
Operating Mines (Form D1)
DMPR-published directory of active mines by province — best free entity list for demo mine names and commodity tags (HTML/PDF; no official CSV).
Minerals Council South Africa
Industry body (formerly Chamber of Mines); Facts & Figures for sales, investment, and policy commentary — complements Stats SA P2041.
Stats SA P2041
Monthly mining production and mineral sales — macro KPI benchmarks for T3/T5 dashboards (PR-07).

SAMREC and SAMVAL — acronym reference

Both codes sit under the SAMCODES Standards Committee (SSC) and are mandatory for JSE-listed mineral companies’ public reporting. They align with the international CRIRSCO family (JORC, NI 43-101, etc.). The 2016 editions remain the current mandatory versions (effective 1 January 2017) — there was no new SAMREC or SAMVAL code release in 2024 or 2025. The SSC runs compliance workshops (e.g. Sept 2025 JSE reporting workshop); a SAMVAL working group is reviewing a future update, but demos should cite the 2016 code until a replacement is published.

SAMREC — resource and reserve reporting

Full name
South African Mineral REC reporting code — The South African Code for the Reporting of Exploration Results, Mineral Resources and Mineral Reserves (2016 edition).
Purpose
Minimum standards for public reporting of exploration results, Mineral Resources (Inferred, Indicated, Measured), and Mineral Reserves (Probable, Proved). Governs Competent Person (CP) sign-off and Table 1 disclosure.
Scope
Solid minerals: base and precious metals, bulk commodities, coal, diamonds/gemstones, industrial minerals, mineral sands. Excludes oil/gas (SAMOG) and water.
Key principles
Materiality, transparency, competency — same philosophical line as JORC. Commodity-specific sections for coal, diamonds, industrial minerals, and metal equivalents.
Coal adjunct
SANS 10320:2017 — national standard for systematic evaluation of coal resources and reserves; referenced by SAMREC coal section.
Demo usage
Use “SAMREC-compliant resource model” when describing block-model or reconciliation inputs; cite Measured/Indicated vs Inferred when explaining forecast uncertainty (T5). Do not invent reserve numbers — point to public CPRs or synthetic specs (PR-07b).

SAMVAL — mineral asset valuation

Full name
South African Mineral VALuation code — The South African Code for the Reporting of Mineral Asset Valuations (2016 edition).
Purpose
Standards for monetary valuation of mineral assets for public reports, transactions, and litigation support. Valuations must be based on resources/reserves prepared under SAMREC (or other CRIRSCO code where justified).
Key principles
Materiality, transparency, competency, plus reasonableness (added in 2016 update). Sign-off by a Competent Mineral Asset Valuator (SAMVAL abbreviation CV — valuation competency, not curriculum vitae; see PR-03 for CV ↔ Ukwazi skill mapping).
Relationship to SAMREC
SAMREC answers “how much metal/geology?”; SAMVAL answers “what is it worth?” — same deposit, different report type. Closure liability and provision work (T6) often references valuation discipline even when not a full SAMVAL report.
Demo usage
T4 techno-economic and T6 closure demos: frame outputs as inputs to a valuation narrative (NPV, discount rate sensitivity) without presenting fake JSE-grade SAMVAL tables.

Operational KPIs — portfolio vocabulary

SA mines use the same core operational KPIs as other jurisdictions; differentiation is in commodity drivers (PGM UG grade-recovery vs coal strip ratio vs iron-ore logistics). Group KPIs by planning horizon and map to portfolio themes.

Production and planning (T1, T5)

KPI Definition / formula SA nuance
Plan attainment % Actual tonnes (or metal) ÷ planned × 100 PGM concentrator feed consistency; coal Eskom nomination vs export split
Strip ratio (W:O) Waste tonnes ÷ ore tonnes Critical in Northern Cape iron ore and Mpumalanga coal open cast
Ring advance / face advance Metres or rings per month (UG) Bushveld UG PGM stoping; bound by seismic and ventilation
Mill feed grade (g/t or %) Head grade to concentrator 4E PGM (Pt, Pd, Rh, Au) reporting common in SA
LOM schedule compliance Cumulative actual vs life-of-mine plan Links short-term schedule (T1) to strategic plan (T5)

Haulage and simulation (T2)

KPI Definition / formula SA nuance
Fleet availability % Available hours ÷ calendar hours Shared contractor fleets on Bushveld mines
Utilisation % Operating hours ÷ available hours Queue at shovel and tip — DES demo targets (PR-05 #01–#03)
Cycle time Load + haul + queue + dump (minutes) Pit-to-plant vs pit-to-stockpile routing
Payload compliance % Actual payload ÷ target payload Under-load hurts cost/t at Sishen-scale pits
BCM/h or tph Bank cubic metres or tonnes per hour moved Contractor payment units in coal

Reconciliation and spatial performance (T3)

KPI Definition / formula SA nuance
Mine call factor (MCF) Payable metal produced ÷ metal called from model (×100%) Gold and PGM operations; MCF >100% or <100% triggers investigation
Dilution / ore loss % Waste in ore / ore left behind UG stope width vs blast design on narrow reefs
Survey-to-plan variance As-built vs design tonnes or metres Core Ukwazi T3 deliverable — spatial accountability
Short- vs long-term model variance Grade/tonnes drift between models SAMREC model update cycles vs operational short-term model
Metallurgical recovery % Metal in product ÷ metal in feed PGM concentrator and chrome spiral circuits

Economics and transition (T4)

KPI Definition / formula SA nuance
Cost per tonne mined / moved / processed Total cost ÷ respective tonnes Deep gold > PGM UG > open-pit bulk ordering on $/t
All-in sustaining cost (AISC) Industry standard gold/PGM unit cost Compare to rand gold price in investor narratives
NPV / IRR Discounted cash flow metrics SAMVAL-aligned sensitivity — not a substitute for formal valuation
IPCC vs trucking $/t Life-cycle cost comparison Iron ore and coal corridor studies (PR-05 #02, #08)

Closure and risk (T6)

KPI Definition / formula SA nuance
Closure provision (R or $) Balance-sheet liability estimate WRC Closure Atlas layers; gold tailings legacy in Gauteng
Water balance / AMD risk Decant prediction, treatment cost WRC Mine Water Atlas — strong public anchor (PR-07)
Tailings dam consequence class GISTM / national dam safety alignment Post-Jagersfontein sensitivity; T6 differentiation

Safety and environment (cross-cutting)

  • LTIFR / TRIFR — lost-time and total recordable injury frequency per million hours; MHSA-driven reporting.
  • Section 54/55 stoppages — inspector-ordered work cessation; hits plan attainment (T1) and utilisation (T2).
  • Dust, noise, water use (m³/t) — environmental licence compliance; secondary to OR demos unless T6 water closure focus.
