## Key findings



- **Deliverable:** `source-natural-resources-handbook-2007.html` — scope, chapter map (32 chapters, mining deep + ag/forestry skim), methods catalog (16 methods), mining deep-read table (13 rows), ag/forestry transfer table (5 rows), five portfolio seeds (A–E), Angular notes, scorecards for top three, artifact scorecard, flagship Seed A sketch.

- **KEY mining OR source:** Part IV (Ch 29–32) is the only Wave 2 text with native open-pit/underground mine case studies and deployed MILP results — unlike Hillier (PR-10) or Boucherie (PR-12).

- **Ch 29 (Caccetta) deep read:** Block model + precedence cones; maximum-weight closure (LGA, Picard flow, PITOPTIM to 3,175,200 blocks); open-pit MILP scheduling with branch-and-cut (≥15% NPV on gold mines; 68 pits / 6 sites / 16-year iron ore); Lagrangian within 5%; GA/SA/neural-net survey; equipment selection and mine rehabilitation refs.

- **Ch 30 (Alford/Brazil/Lee) deep read:** Underground sublevel open stoping and sublevel caving; infill drilling optimisation (Pajingo 10% savings); Lane cut-off grade; stope optimisation (Floating Stope, conditional simulation grade risk); gradient-constrained Steiner decline networks; underground scheduling RCPSP survey including Kiruna precursor and Whittle (2004) SA.

- **Ch 31 (Kiruna) deep read:** LKAB sublevel caving — 24 Mt/y, 3 ore products, machine placements with vertical/horizontal sequencing and drawdown lines; combined long/short-term MILP minimizing contract deviation; critical-path variable elimination (4,000+ binaries → ~20%); 69.6% deviation reduction vs long-term-only; 4 s vs 4,350 s solve time at 5% gap on 24-month data.

- **Ch 32 (copper chain) deep read:** Sequential mine→plant→investment planning vs integrated vision; hardness/recovery/pollutant nonlinearities; smelter-refinery logistics; water (53% at crush-flotation) and energy (48%) as binding limits; cites Codelco/Epstein and cross-refs Kiruna.

- **Ag/forestry skim (method transfer only):** Ag Ch 7 simulation→MCDM; forestry Ch 26 SA harvest scheduling (ODF 300k–900k binaries); Ch 28 adaptive LP+simulation — parallel to mine rolling-horizon and metaheuristic scheduling when MILP intractable.

- **Top seeds (composite):** A — Kiruna combined MILP (T1, 3.86); C — conditional simulation stope risk (T2/T5, 3.78); B — Caccetta branch-and-cut open-pit scheduling (T1, 3.55). D — integrated copper chain (~3.24); E — decline Steiner network (~3.08) below threshold; kept for T4/T6 breadth. A prioritises T1; C prioritises T2/T5 (not T1).

- **T6 adjacency:** Ch 32 water/energy/pollutant limits and Ch 29 mine rehabilitation refs support PR-09/PR-05 closure depth notes without a dedicated T6 seed.



## Open questions / risks



- **Industrial MILP scale:** Ch 29 branch-and-cut and Ch 31 full Kiruna model exceed browser Worker — demos must use toy instances + variable elimination (Ch 31.4) or stay narrative with Cloud Function.

- **LKAB data confidentiality:** Published structure (Interfaces 34(2)) sufficient for synthetic replication; exact block grades not in handbook text.

- **Ch 32 integration gap:** Authors state no published integrated mine-metallurgy MILP — Seed D is vision + toy LP, not reproduced industrial model.

- **Conditional simulation complexity:** Ch 30.3.3 cites case study but not step-by-step algorithm — Seed C needs simplified Floating Stope heuristic on PR-07b realisations.
