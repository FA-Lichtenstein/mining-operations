SME desk reference (~74k sentencized lines, 26 chapters) of conversion factors, material properties, statistics, economics, project evaluation, mining methods, haulage formulas, processing, tailings, maintenance, and closure bonding. Unlike narrative Dunbar (PR-15) or MIS-focused Sloan (PR-14), this handbook is the formula and KPI-definition anchor for portfolio demos — availability × utilization, truck match factor, effective grade, NPV/IRR study ladders, method-selection matrices, tailings embankment constraints, and bonding phase release. Five seeds below; top three by composite: A → C → B (all ≥ 3.5). Seed A is the flagship fleet KPI calculator.

1. Scope & audience

  • Text: 2nd ed. (SME, 2020). 26 chapters + index. Concise reference for everyday engineering calculations — not a textbook narrative or optimisation treatise.
  • Audience: Quantitative Planning Analyst portfolio — citeable equations, KPI labels, and constraint thresholds for browser calculators and interview fluency; complements Dunbar vocabulary and Sloan reconciliation patterns.
  • Role in Wave 2: Primary operational formula catalog. Use for Worker algebra, tooltip definitions, and study-stage accuracy bands before citing MILP/DES from PR-13 or Gorai (PR-17).
  • Cross-refs: SA demo site profiles (PR-09); synthetic fleet/grade data (PR-07b); stack patterns (PR-04); Dunbar strip ratio / cut-off narrative (PR-15); Sloan avail/util variance (PR-14 Table 6.4).

5. Hiring-manager & demo language

  • “Fleet plans should separate availability (maintenance readiness) from utilization (active production time) — operating efficiency is their product.” (Ch 12 Eq 12.14; Ch 22 definitions)
  • “Truck fleet sizing is a match-factor problem: cycle time divided by load time, with spares for breakdowns.” (Ch 12 Eq 12.27)
  • “Haul road resistance shows up as effective grade — packed gravel adds ~2.3% equivalent grade before you account for physical ramp.” (Ch 12 Table 12.2; Eq 12.9)
  • “Study-stage economics carry explicit accuracy bands — ±50% capital at scoping (Table 9.1), ±15% at feasibility; narrative also cites ±20% bankable FS — PEA is not a bankable decision document.” (Ch 9 Table 9.1; Ch 9 text)
  • “Reserve due diligence checks cutoff grade assumptions, dilution, grade control method, and reconciliation of ore produced to the geologic model.” (Ch 9 Table 9.2)
  • “Method selection balances recovery vs dilution — block cave 90%/20% vs cut-and-fill 100%/0% — against productivity and geotechnical risk.” (Ch 10 Table 10.4)
  • “Tailings staging must track the mine plan; upstream raises above 5 m/yr are a red flag for stability.” (Ch 21)
  • “Closure liability includes progressive bond release phases and contingent environmental obligations, not just end-of-life capex.” (Ch 25)

7. Portfolio seeds

Five seeds tagged T1–T6; handbook supplies formulas and KPI definitions, not algorithms. Top three by composite: A → C → B (all ≥ 3.5). Seeds D and E (~3.54) also clear threshold — D for T6 breadth, E for T3/T5 grade QA.

Seed A — Fleet operating efficiency & match-factor calculator T2

Source: Ch 12 Eqs 12.9, 12.14, 12.17, 12.27; Table 12.2 rolling resistance; Table 12.3 speed derating; Ch 13 stopping-distance limit.

Idea: Inputs: shovel cycle time, truck load/spot time, haul profile (grade + surface type), availability A and utilization U. Worker computes E, match factor Nh, implied queue risk, and effective grade. Optional constraint flags when stopping distance exceeds 200 ft at design speed. Flagship formula demo — pairs Sloan Table 6.4 avail/util accountability (PR-14).

Seed B — Study-stage NPV & accuracy-band sensitivity lab T4 T5

Source: Ch 8 Eq 8.27, Tables 8.9–8.10; Ch 9 Table 9.1 (PEA/PFS/FS capital ±50/25/15%, opex ±35/25/15%, contingency 25/15/10%).

Idea: Toggle study stage → auto-apply accuracy bands to capex/opex inputs; slider price, grade, recovery; NPV/IRR cards with warning when user selects PEA-grade inputs for “bankable” label. Techno-economic literacy demo for planning interviews.

Seed C — Mining method trade-off explorer T4

Source: Ch 10 Table 10.4 (relative cost, selectivity, recovery/dilution %, productivity t/employee, environment/safety); Ch 10 pushback/SR narrative.

Idea: Interactive matrix: filter methods by deposit type (tabular/massive), rank by recovery–dilution trade-off and relative cost index. Tooltip hiring-manager lines from §5. Complements Dunbar method narrative (PR-15 Table 2.4) with quantified handbook indices.

Seed D — Tailings staging & bond-release timeline T6

Source: Ch 21 Table 21.1 siting parameters; embankment types Fig 21.2; raise-rate constraint; Ch 25 SMCRA Phase I–III bond release; environmental liability checklist.

Idea: Scenario toggles: upstream vs centerline vs downstream; climate/seismic/social proximity from Table 21.1. Timeline Gantt for bond phases; flag when mine-plan change should trigger TSF review. Pairs Dunbar tailings narrative (PR-15 Seed D) with regulatory/formula depth.

Seed E — Grade confidence & COG due-diligence checklist T3 T5

Source: Ch 3 confidence intervals (Table 3.9); Ch 9 kriging/semivariogram; Table 9.2 items (cutoff grade assumptions, dilution, grade control, model validation).

Idea: Sample-size calculator for grade mean CI; synthetic block model with kriging-error bands; interactive due-diligence checklist with RAG status per Table 9.2 mining section. T3 reconciliation literacy without proprietary block models.
