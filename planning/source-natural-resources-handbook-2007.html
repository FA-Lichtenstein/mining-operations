Springer ISORMS handbook (Hillier series; Weintraub/Romero/Epstein editors) spanning agriculture, fisheries, forestry, and mining. Part IV (Ch 29–32) is the portfolio’s only mining-native OR source in Wave 2: Caccetta on open-pit pit design and branch-and-cut scheduling (≥15% NPV uplift on operating gold mines); Alford/Brazil/Lee on underground stope, network, and scheduling decomposition; Newman/Kuchta/Martinez on LKAB Kiruna combined long/short-term MILP (69.6% contract-deviation reduction); and Caro/Epstein/Weintraub on integrated copper mine-metallurgy chain planning. Deep read centred on T1 scheduling and T2 stochastic/simulation threads; ag/forestry skimmed for LP+simulation and metaheuristic scheduling transfer only. Five seeds below; top three by composite: A → C → B (all ≥ 3.5). Seed A anchors T1; Seed C anchors T2/T5 simulation.

1. Scope & audience

  • Text: Edited handbook, Springer 2007. ~36k sentencized lines; 32 chapters across four domains + index. Part IV mining (pp. 545–609) is four application chapters with published mine case studies — unlike Hillier (PR-10) or Boucherie (PR-12).
  • Audience: Quantitative Planning Analyst portfolio — citeable mining MILP/scheduling/simulation vocabulary for browser demos per PR-04; complements Gorai mining formulations (PR-17) with industry deployment narratives (LKAB Interfaces, Codelco, Caccetta branch-and-cut).
  • Read boundary: Deep Ch 29 (block model, Lerchs–Grossmann/max-closure, MILP scheduling, branch-and-cut); Ch 30 (infill drilling, cut-off, stope optimisation, Steiner decline networks, Kiruna precursor, conditional simulation); Ch 31 (Kiruna MILP, variable reduction, numerical results); Ch 32 (copper chain integration, hardness/recovery nonlinearity, water/energy limits). Skim ag Ch 1 (farm risk), Ch 7 (MCDM+simulation), Ch 9 (PMP calibration); forestry Ch 22 (harvest ops), Ch 26 (heuristics/SA scheduling), Ch 28 (LP+simulation strategy). TOC only: fisheries, remaining ag/forestry.
  • Cross-refs: Hillier Ch 10 network, Ch 12 IP (PR-10); haulage DES patterns (PR-05); SA iron-ore personas (PR-09); synthetic block models (PR-07b); Epstein/Weintraub also author forest transport Ch 22 and copper Ch 32.

Seed C — Conditional simulation stope grade risk T2 T5

Idea: Generate N synthetic block models (PR-07b); run Floating-Stope-style envelope on each; histogram of stope NPV or grade. Compare deterministic vs P10/P50/P90 stope value — T2 simulation pillar with published mining citation (not generic DES).

Seed D — Integrated copper mine-metallurgy chain T4 T6

Source: Ch 32.1–2 (sequential vs integrated); Ch 32.4.1 (hardness Wi→tpd, recovery vs µm, pollutant blend); Ch 32.4.2.2 (water 53% / energy 48% at crushing-grinding-flotation, Tables 1–2).

Idea: Two-mine, one-concentrator toy LP/MILP: monthly ore feed by hardness class affects mill tpd and recovery; water cap binds in dry-site persona (PR-09). Vision doc + small solver — integration gap is the story (Ch 32.5).

Seed E — Underground decline Steiner network T4

Source: Ch 30.3.4–3.5 (gradient-constrained Steiner trees; max slope m=1/7–1/9; Dubins navigability); cross-ref Ch 30.3.1 Pajingo infill drilling network case.
